James Mountford is an active saxophonist, performer, and educator in the Chicagoland area, currently pursuing a degree in Music Education from DePaul University in Chicago, Illinois. At DePaul, James primarily studies saxophone with Jeremy Ruthrauff and Scott Burns. He is also a member of the DePaul Wind Ensemble, Ensemble 20+, and multiple chamber ensembles.

Prior to his time at DePaul, James spent time studying under Dr. Paul Nolen at Illinois State University from 2019-2021, and was a member of the Symphonic Winds, Wind Symphony, Big Red Marching Machine, and chamber ensembles among others. As a part of the saxophone studio, James also traveled to and performed at the 2020 North American Saxophone Alliance in Tempe, AZ.

During the summer of 2018, James was also a participant in the 2018 ISYM camp and studied saxophone with Dr. Debra Richtmeyer and Dr. Connie Frigo. Also in that year, James was the saxophone section leader and on the leadership team for the Lincoln-Way Marching Band. There, he helped lead the band to a state championship at the 2018 Illinois State Marching Band Championships, a 4th place finish at the Bands of America St. Louis Super Regional, and a performance at the Rose Parade in Pasadena, California, on Jan 1st, 2019.

Academically, James successfully auditioned for and was accepted to the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign, Illinois State University, and DePaul University. Ultimately, James attended ISU during 2019-2021 before transferring to DePaul, with an anticipated graduation date of December 2023. James received significant merit & talent scholarships from both institutions he attended.

Significant teachers of James also include Dr. Greg Rife & Mr. Rob Denty. James is a recipient of the John Philip Sousa & Arion band awards. He is a member of the Illinois Music Education Assocation, the North American Saxophone Alliance, and the National Association for Music Education. James is also a backing instrumentalist for the band BAJA.
